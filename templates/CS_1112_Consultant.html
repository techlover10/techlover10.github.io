Overview

CS 1112 at Cornell University is an introductory computer science course which uses MATLAB to teach the basics of computational thinking and problem-solving with software development. Students are introduced to the basics of control flow, sorting algorithms, and developing problem-solving with programming. This course is one of two introductory Computer Science courses commonly used by students in the College of Engineering to satisfy their Computer Science graduation requirement.

Responsibilities

  • Grade prelim exams (two per semester), projects (6 per semester) and the final exam.
  • Hold weekly office hours to provide assistance to students with projects and assignments, and answer questions about course material.
  • Answer questions and help the TAs teach course material in weekly discussion sessions of approx. 20-25 students.
